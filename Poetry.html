My Poetry

Poem 1

Lovers take time
Time is greater than distance
Distance is but a perpetual precautionary self defense mechanism
Mechanism like my heart: a beating contraption whose pumping I have yet to find an off switch for
For you to laugh when you can’t swim is for me to wish nails could carve through cement
Cement weaker than the glass walls you build around the reality of hunger
Hunger for oceans to sink and ship wrecks to float as if there were never any wars at our seas
Seas swallowing footsteps on the shores of your already limited fantasies
Fantasies that we dreamt of when younger were once perriwinkle
Periwinkle like the shade of convincing ourselves that we can meld pebbles into planets
Planets stand in between us but we still shove universes down our throats and regurgitate it into each other
Other people would not understand the determination to keep my hand folded in your palms
Palms of terror festering under my skin
Skin is supposed to be soft so don’t ask me why mine isn’t
Isn’t wonderful to get answers to your questions
Questions only dig ravines into our conversations when I bite honesty into my cheek and drain the blood from my blush
Blush is a pretty shade on you that I’m glad I can paint for distraction
Distraction is the sweetest salvation, recovery from fights we never had, that sugary flavor in the back of our tongues
Tongues drooping past our chins, we are exhausted from drooling lies


Poem 2
Brewing coffee in the garden of eden
Slowly stirred with milk from cattle we raised beforehand
The cream felt heavenly on our tongues as we drank our confessions
Holiness was not what we were scavenging for
We simply considered each other distracting
Love is momentary anyways
And later vineyard grapes would sour sweet words
Sip tasteless wine after caffeine
Energy from yesterday morning finally shatters and we blame the other one for dropping it
Shards of euphoric reluctance on the ground and we wait to see who sweeps it up
Pigeons flutter past rusted metal fences
We dreamt clandestine alleys were worlds that slipped through God’s fingertips
Yet only humanity’s eyes etched embarrassment onto our sins


Poem 3
Beauty is only sufficient if it is elastic
Look dazzling when you cry
Anger needs to have a glimmer to it
Anxiety should be dressed elegantly
Fear needs to stunning, but in a subtle way
Pain is gorgeous, blindingly
Sleepless nights are alluring
Bite marks on your lips are decorative
Turn flushed skin into a delicate blush
Paint your nails with any dismay received
Embarrassment must be worn proudly
After all, it’s custom made
The crashing sound of the divergence between your eye color in summer’s child and the words you cut your tongue with needs to be melodic
The shades of your skin need to shine alongside stars
The darkened patches beneath your eyes need to be comforting

Poem 4
Sipping from broken glasses
Filling Sunday mornings with the smell of split lips
Honesty tars you darling
Puncture you like paper and you crumple in my hands
Anticipation heals idealism as if they’re open cuts
Ink stains of distorted regrets spilling through fabric
Infectious exposure traced back to self dialogue
I’m choking over blame I haven’t built
Double tonguing I love yous
Overlapping declarations blurring into misunderstandings
I’m brewing frustration over still stares
Flush my fears down your throat
Clean my wounds with consolidation
Kiss the shards on the floors we’re tiptoeing on
I’m craving numbness and you’re keeping me starved

Poem 5
I’m trying to convey to you my reasoning
But my tongue’s twisting yields no results
I talk through wanting
Listen to the way my hands sadly trace your skin
Fingertips mumbling white lies

Poem 6
Overreaction overflowing in my direction
Breathing through scarce air bubbles
It’s secure to you how blame is cancerous and you’re blissfully immune
Hanging me by the rope of my crumbling stability
Yet I’ll only learn to be responsive through resurrection
Crucify the cracks in my voice but it’s when the wood is rotting that my remorse thins out

Poem 7
I am standing on the coast
Slowly deepening my toes into the damp shore,
My arms totter like the branches of a sapling being bullied by the winds
I promise, I’d scream for your name, but the sand settles down my throat oh so easily and weighs my vocals down into the pit of my stomach
I can feel it thinning in the acid
It’s terrifying to say I love you from afar anyways
I am standing on the coast
I sharpen my vision to see you - not too far away
Not too far away, but a universe fills in the space between us
Only miles apart, I can feel your breath whisper past the tip of my ears, but the distance is perpetual
You’re so close, cradling me away, but the obsidian riptides will chase your ankles and grab your shins to tear you back
I stretch my forearm across the ocean, let it graze the sea salt
The waves flap over my inner elbow weighing my shoulders down and I can feel planets on my fingertips
Stardust gets trapped underneath my nails
There is a solar system in between us
I am standing on the coast
You are light years away, but I can almost embrace you and tell you that you have flown to me and that you will stay
I am safe and sound on the coast where I will always be warm
I am sunbathing on the coast and the sun shines here more than people understand
I watch this world take for granted how soft sand can be compared to the gravel that you are held on barefoot
I am standing on the coast, and no matter how far you’ll sail, how much you’ll kiss the shore when you arrive, and how long you’ll stay in the sun, you will always be tied to an infinity away
I was born on this coast, where I will always be waiting
And I could only ever love half of you
But you’ll be standing on that damn coast
Coming
but you'll never be leaving
